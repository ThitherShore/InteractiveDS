Is the data coherent?

Does it make sense relative to itself? Does it match your expectations? Are the distributions sensible? What is your evidence for this?

Answer. Yes. From the statistical perspective, the sum of each row is 100%, since the data is used to describe the market share held by worldwide smartphone vendors. There is no extreme value, or any value greater than 1 or less 0. These data are all consistent with the contents they are describing and thus reasonable.

In addition, from my perspective, the trend described by the table is consistent with my knowledge: Apple and Samsung are increasingly gaining popularity while Nokia is gradually losing its market share. So it matches up quite well well with my expectation.

This dataset describes the trend of market share held by but smartphone vendors rather than some kind of distribution. So I would answer that the trend is sensible.

Is the data correct?

What aspects of this data can you verify? How did you do so? What did you find?

Answer. 

It doesn't require complicated measurements or any objective conjectures to obtain the data, ruling out possible errors caused by measurements, question designs, implementations and so on.

Though based on the discussion above (my expectation, and the sum of 1) and the authoritative data source, the data is correct with a very high chance, we still cannot rule out the chances of regional sample bias since it is supposed to be based on a worldwide survey.
