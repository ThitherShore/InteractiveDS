Distribution of Astraptes Fulgerator Complex Samples

Description

The map below demonstrates the distribution of Astraptes Fulgerator Complex from public data downloaded from here. The demonstration was achieved through Google Fusion Table.

Detailed Information

You could find further information about each sample by clicking the marker on the map, then you could find the picture of each sample, what herbivore species it belongs to, it's voucher, and the detailed information below (for example) as well.

  • sex: female
  • host plant species: Senna hayesiana
  • host plant family: Fabaceae
  • year: 2003
  • date eclosion: 7/27/03
  • elevation: 480
  • wingspan (mm): 61
  • primary eco: rain forest
  • latitude: 11.00766
  • longitude: -85.47926

Markers

I applied different marker icons to distinguish those samples of different windspan. Specifically, I split the samples into 4 subsets with regard to windspan (mm):

  • A) 43.0 ~ 43.1 (super-small): yellow
  • B) 43.1 ~ 50.0 (small) : green
  • C) 50.0 ~ 60.0 (medium) : blue
  • D) 60.0 ~ 70.0 (large) : purple

Sets A) is simply designed to mark the one with smallest windspan for visualization purpose.

Question

Is there any regional difference on the wingspan of Astraptes Fulgerator Complex? That is to day, by looking at the information provided by the map below, is the length of windspan related to the location (altitude, longtitude) of the Astraptes Fulgerator Complex?

Note: Since this web page provides better visual information than quantitative or statistical information, I chose to ask the question about the visual part.

Answer

Useful Data 

Splitting the sample into 4 subsets with regard to the length of windspan and annotating each subset with a single color provide useful information to answer this question. That is to say, the annotation is informative for answering this question.

Answer

There are some regional differences. That's because all Astraptes Fulgerator Complex with larger windspan (in this dataset), except the one of smallest windspan, are in the east part, and those of small windspan are in the west part, though blue (medium) sets are distributed among the whole region. However, the number of this dataset is too limited to draw any definitive conclusion about whether the difference is significant or negligible basing on this map demonstration.
